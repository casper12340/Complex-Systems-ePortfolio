Data Description

The dataset used for this ePortfolio is the Kaggle "Massive Yahoo Finance Dataset" created by Yahoo Finance Dataset. The data was collected from Yahoo Finance and includes the top 500 companies by market capitalisation from 29 November 2018 to 29 November 2023. In total, this dataset consists of more than 600,000 records, with each row representing a trading day for one of the companies. The data is structured as a multivariate time series, since it tracks multiple variables over time for each company. It shows how the companies performed on a daily basis. The dataset includes the following attributes:

  • Date: The date on which the stock price data was recorded.
  • Open: The price of the stock when the selected stock opened on this date.
  • High: The highest price of the stock on this date.
  • Low: The stock's lowest price on this date.
  • Close: The price of the stock when the stock marked closed on this date.
  • Volume: The number of stocks traded on this date.
  • Dividends: Dividend payments made by the company on this date (if applicable).
  • Stock Splits: Information on any stock splits that have occurred on that date.
  • Company: The name of the company of which the data is.

This dataset is relevant to our portfolio because it shows a real-world complex system which can be used to analyse and understand. The large number of data together with the continuity makes this dataset useful for studying stock market trends, exploring relationships between different variables, and forecasting future stock prices. Stock market data provides an excellent opportunity to explore time-series analysis and dynamical systems concepts. In this document, we analyse historical stock price data for GOOGL (Google) over five years. Using statistical, visualization, and nonlinear techniques, we uncover insights into price fluctuations and their underlying structure.
